Progressive Stamping Die Assembly Design

Parametric Mechanical Tooling Assembly

This project involves the design of a progressive stamping die assembly intended for repeatable sheet-metal forming operations in a production press environment. The assembly integrates upper and lower die shoes, guide pillars, bushings, punches, strippers, and spring-loaded elements into a rigid and precisely aligned tooling system.

The design emphasizes precision alignment, controlled motion, and serviceability. Guide pillars and bushings maintain vertical alignment throughout the press stroke, while spring-loaded stripper components manage material release and controlled return motion. Fastener placement and component spacing were optimized to allow maintenance and part replacement without full disassembly.

All components were modeled using fully parametric parts and constraint-based assembly relationships to accurately represent press motion and mechanical interactions. Interference, clearance, and alignment checks were performed across the full operating range to validate tooling behavior prior to manufacturing.

  • Progressive die layout, tooling fundamentals, spring-loaded mechanisms
  • Assembly modeling
  • Manufacturing Tooling
  • Professional CAD organization

Spring-Loaded Tooling

Fixture Mechanism

This project demonstrates a spring-loaded tooling mechanism designed to control linear motion, preload, and return force within a mechanical assembly. Compression springs are integrated with guide posts to ensure smooth, repeatable motion while preventing lateral deflection.

Sliding components are supported by flat bearing surfaces designed to distribute loads evenly. The assembly is modular, allowing adjustment and maintenance without redesign.

The project highlights practical mechanical design principles commonly used in tooling, fixturing, and press-related systems.

Rear Wheel Assembly Design

Drive, Hub & Braking Integration

This project represents a complete rear wheel assembly, integrating the wheel, hub, axle, bearings, sprocket, braking disc, and fasteners into a unified mechanical system.

The design demonstrates coordination between rotational components, torque transmission, braking interfaces, and structural support. Bearings manage radial and axial loads while maintaining alignment. The sprocket transmits drivetrain torque concentrically through the hub, while the brake disc is isolated on the opposite side for controlled deceleration.

Exploded views illustrate assembly order, fastener grouping, bearing placement, and service access. The design allows disassembly of major subsystems without disturbing the entire assembly.

  • Rotational system design, bearing integration,assembly logic
  • Exploded-view documentation
  • Drivetrain and braking layout
  • Serviceability-focused CAD
